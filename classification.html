Domain Classification

The question

How do new species get classified today? How has taxonomy evolved with genetic technology?

Scientists classify living things to organize them based on their similarities and differences. The broadest level is called a domain, and all life is divided into three domains — Bacteria, Archaea, and Eukarya. Bacteria and Archaea are made of cells without a nucleus, while Eukarya includes animals, plants, fungi, and protists, whose cells have a nucleus (NASA, 2001).

Levels of classification

Domain → Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species

In the past, scientists mostly classified organisms by comparing physical traits, such as their body shape and structures. Today, scientists can also use DNA sequencing and DNA barcoding to compare genetic information. This helps them figure out how closely organisms are related and whether an organism may be a new species. Genetic technology has also caused scientists to change some older classifications when DNA evidence showed that certain organisms were more or less closely related than scientists originally thought (Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History, n.d.).
