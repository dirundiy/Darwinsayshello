About Darwin

Charles Darwin was an English naturalist who developed the theory of evolution by natural selection. He was born on February 12, 1809, in Shrewsbury, England. From 1831 to 1836, Darwin traveled around the world on the HMS Beagle, where he observed many different plants, animals, fossils, and environments. His observations, especially of the finches on the Galápagos Islands, helped him realize that living things can change over time as they adapt to their surroundings.

He said that organisms with helpful traits are more likely to survive, reproduce, and pass those traits on to their offspring. In 1859, Darwin published On the Origin of Species, where he explained his theory of evolution by natural selection. His discoveries changed the way scientists understand the diversity of life and remain one of the foundations of modern biology.
