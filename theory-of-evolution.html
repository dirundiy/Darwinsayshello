Background

Theory of Evolution

The theory of evolution states that all living organisms gradually change over long periods of time. Charles Darwin explained that these changes happen through a process called natural selection. Within every species, individuals have different inherited traits, and some of these traits make it easier for them to survive and reproduce in their environment. As these individuals have offspring, their useful traits are passed down and become more common in future generations.

Over many generations, these small changes can result in the formation of new species while others may disappear. Darwin developed this idea after studying plants, animals, and fossils during his journey on the HMS Beagle, particularly on the Galápagos Islands.

Today, scientists continue to support the theory of evolution with evidence from fossils, DNA, comparative anatomy, and observations of living organisms.
